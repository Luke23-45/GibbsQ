\subsection{Phase~1: Behavior Cloning}

The BC phase initializes the neural policy by supervised imitation of an analytical teacher~\cite{ross2011reduction}.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\small
\caption{Behavior cloning configuration.}
\label{tab:bc_config}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.25}
\begin{tabular}{@{\hspace{6pt}}l @{\hspace{20pt}} r@{\hspace{6pt}}}
\toprule
\textbf{Parameter} & \textbf{Value} \\
\midrule
Expert policy         & UAS ($\temp = 10.0$) \\
Preprocessing         & $\log(1 + \Qlen{i})$ \\
Training steps        & 1{,}000 \\
Final accuracy        & 99.2\% (best seed, 97--99\% inter-seed range) \\
Loss                  & Cross-entropy \\
Optimizer             & Adam \\
Learning rate         & $3 \times 10^{-4}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The BC teacher generates routing decisions for randomly sampled queue states.
The network is uniquely trained to minimize the cross-entropy between its modeled softmax output and the teacher's action distribution. The final fidelity metric actively captures the accuracy of the multi-class full routing distribution, not solely the argmax.
At convergence, the network broadly reproduces the teacher's routing distribution (evaluated identically post-hoc at 99.2\% top-1 accuracy for the best seed, with an inter-seed range of 97--99\%), providing a distributionally stable initialization for Phase~2.

For the \textbf{calibrated-teacher} ablation variant, the behavior cloning configuration is strictly identical to Table~\ref{tab:bc_config}, with the sole exception that the expert policy is swapped to Calibrated UAS $(0.85, 0.5, 0.5)$. The exact same network architecture, preprocessing, learning rate, and optimizer settings are applied, yielding comparable target fidelity.

\subsection{Phase~2: REINFORCE Fine-Tuning}

Starting from the BC-pretrained weights, we apply the \reinforce{} policy gradient algorithm~\cite{williams1992simple} to directly optimize the steady-state mean total queue length.
To mitigate the known sensitivity of \reinforce{} to small batches and trajectory randomness, the primary model is trained across 5 independent random initialization seeds. As noted in the main empirical section, the reported metrics in the formal evaluation reflect robust testing (32 independent inference replications) evaluated using the strongest candidate policy from this training ensemble.

Each training epoch collects 16 episodes of queue simulation, each running for 1{,}000 time units under the current policy.
The policy gradient is estimated using the \reinforce{} score-function estimator with a running-average baseline to reduce variance.
No gradient clipping or entropy regularization bonus is applied during training; the BC initialization provides sufficient stability to avoid catastrophic divergence.
Convergence is typically observed by epoch 10--12, with the deterministic policy score plateauing thereafter. To ensure this was not premature convergence, we extended empirical evaluation loops to 30 epochs on a subset of seeds and observed no further significant deviations in loss or mean queue length, verifying 15 epochs as a sufficient optimization horizon for this architecture.
The 32.6\% magnitude error relative to finite-difference gradients (Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_gradient}) effectively reduces the learning rate but does not affect the optimization direction.

\subsection{Ablation Variant Descriptions}

The ablation study (Section~\ref{sec:exp:ablation}) evaluates five training variants to isolate the dominant design factors.
Each variant modifies exactly one aspect of the default pipeline.

  & Initializes the final (output) layer weights to zero before BC training. Tests whether the initialization of the policy head affects downstream performance. \\

  & Skips BC entirely and trains from random initialization. Tests whether BC pretraining is necessary, or whether \reinforce{} alone can discover good routing policies. \\

The key finding is that teacher choice ($1.32$-unit improvement) dominates all architectural modifications ($< 0.2$-unit effects), while training from scratch without BC diverges catastrophically (mean queue $> 5{,}000$).

\section{Piecewise Lyapunov Candidate Search}
\label{sec:appendix_piecewise_search}

The mathematically formulated piecewise Lyapunov candidate presented in Section~\ref{sec:caluas_piecewise_candidate} (Equation 67) was numerically discovered using an automated post-validation search procedure. The configuration evaluates a heavily parameterized convex combination scalarizing various structural components bounding the state space:
1. Base heterogeneous quadratic bound ($\frac{1}{2} \sum_i \Qlen{i}^2 / \mu_i$)
2. Unweighted state limits $(\sum_i \Qlen{i})^2$
3. Smoothed polynomial queue bounds ($\sum_i \Qlen{i}^3 / \mu_i$)
4. Maximum-queue polynomial bounds ($\max_i \Qlen{i}^2$)

The scalar numerical coefficients were jointly optimized using L-BFGS-B minimization to strictly minimize the supremum of the CTMC generator $\gen \lyap_{\mathrm{PW}}(\Qstate)$ evaluated exactly across the state space shells structurally bounding normal-load stationarity ($|\Qstate|_1 \in \{12, \dots, 20\}$). Counter-example tail limit states that repeatedly violated negative boundary drift bounds during concurrent random-check loop verifications were dynamically prioritized in the search weight evaluation matrix. The final convergence weights determining the precise mixture were extracted and locked to 6 decimal precision points explicitly for robust empirical capsule testing.
