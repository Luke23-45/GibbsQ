
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Routing jobs to heterogeneous parallel servers is a central problem in operations research, arising in server farms, cloud platforms, manufacturing lines, and communication networks~\cite{weber1978optimal,winston1977optimality}.
The classical Join the Shortest Queue (JSQ) policy is optimal under server symmetry but ignores service-rate heterogeneity; conversely, proportional-to-capacity routing exploits heterogeneity but discards queue-state information entirely.
We address this gap by designing routing laws that are simultaneously \emph{state-dependent}, \emph{capacity-aware}, and analytically tractable for stability analysis.

\subsection{Entropy-Regularized Routing}
We adopt a Gibbs-measure perspective: rather than routing deterministically to the ``best'' server, we distribute arrivals via a softmax over server scores, regularized by an entropy or KL-divergence penalty.
The temperature parameter $\temp > 0$ interpolates smoothly between exploratory (uniform) and exploitative (greedy) routing.
This formulation connects queue routing to convex duality --- the log-sum-exp function is the convex conjugate of the negative entropy --- and yields smooth, differentiable routing probabilities amenable to gradient-based optimization.

\subsubsection{Stability Theorems}
We prove positive Harris recurrence for two entropy-regularized routing laws under the strict load condition $\totcap > \arrate$:
(i)~\emph{raw softmax}, via a five-step Foster--Lyapunov argument with a quadratic Lyapunov function (Theorem~\ref{thm:softmax}), and
(ii)~the \emph{Unified Archimedean Softmax} (UAS), via a weighted Lyapunov proof that exploits a prior-weighted KL variational identity (Theorem~\ref{thm:uas}).
Both proofs yield explicit drift constants.

\subsubsection{Calibrated Closed-Form Policy}
We introduce Calibrated UAS, a three-parameter empirical generalization of UAS.
In the common evaluation protocol ($\Nservers{=}10$, $\rho{=}0.80$, 32 replications), it achieves $\E[\sum_i \Qlen{i}] = 10.04$, outperforming JSSQ ($11.02$), UAS ($11.50$), and JSQ ($11.81$).

\subsubsection{Learned Policy via Structured Ablation}
We establish that \emph{teacher-policy choice} is the dominant factor in learned-policy quality.
A neural policy initialized via behavior cloning from Calibrated UAS and fine-tuned with \reinforce{} achieves $9.82$, whereas the same architecture trained from UAS achieves $11.14$, and training from scratch diverges catastrophically ($5{,}451$).

The learned-policy advantage generalizes across all 16 tested operating-regime configurations and persists under near-critical load up to $\rho = 0.98$.
