
\begin{abstract}
Routing policies for heterogeneous parallel queues face a fundamental tension between state-awareness and capacity-awareness. While classical policies typically prioritize only one, and reinforcement learning (RL) approaches lack formal stability, we propose GibbsQ---a framework that bridges this gap via entropy-regularized routing.

At the \textbf{analytical level}, we formally prove positive Harris recurrence for three routing laws: raw softmax, UAS (under the strict load condition $\totcap > \arrate$), and a stability-certified calibrated subclass (SCUAS) under an explicit parameter-conditional drift bound. These proofs yield computable drift constants; notably, the UAS constants are temperature-independent.

At the \textbf{empirical level}, we introduce Calibrated UAS, a closed-form extension of UAS with three tunable parameters. We find that while Calibrated UAS delivers superior performance---reducing steady-state queue lengths by 8.9\% and 12.6\% relative to JSSQ and baseline UAS respectively---the benchmark-default setting is not theorem-certified. A post-validation audit confirms that the audited benchmark configuration yields negative sufficient drift constants, thus these results remain empirical rather than certified in this study. However, we identify a benchmark-specific piecewise Lyapunov candidate that materially narrows the case for future certification.

Finally, we observe a \textbf{teacher-choice effect} in the learned-policy layer. An RL agent initialized from Calibrated UAS and fine-tuned via \reinforce{} attains the minimum queue length among all evaluated policies, outperforming even its closed-form teacher. This performance gain is strictly directional under controlled evaluation and does not achieve statistical significance, functioning primarily to identify behavioral potential and motivate advanced variance reduction techniques. However, the directional advantage persists across 16 operating configurations and into near-critical regimes up to $\rho = 0.98$.
\end{abstract}
